As the incidence of immune-mediated diseases has increased rapidly in developed societies, there is an unmet need      License 4.0 (CC BY).
for novel prophylactic practices to fight against these maladies. This study is the first human intervention trial in
which urban environmental biodiversity was manipulated to examine its effects on the commensal microbiome
and immunoregulation in children. We analyzed changes in the skin and gut microbiota and blood immune mark-
ers of children during a 28-day biodiversity intervention. Children in standard urban and nature-oriented daycare
centers were analyzed for comparison. The intervention diversified both the environmental and skin Gammapro-
teobacterial communities, which, in turn, were associated with increases in plasma TGF-1 levels and the propor-
tion of regulatory T cells. The plasma IL-10:IL-17A ratio increased among intervention children during the trial.
Our findings suggest that biodiversity intervention enhances immunoregulatory pathways and provide an in-
centive for future prophylactic approaches to reduce the risk of immune-mediated diseases in urban societies.
